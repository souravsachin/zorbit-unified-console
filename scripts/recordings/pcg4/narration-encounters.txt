The PCG4 encounter taxonomy is the backbone of every product configuration. It defines forty-four encounter types organized across ten clinical categories. Preventive Care includes annual physicals, immunizations, cancer screenings, and wellness visits — these are typically covered at one hundred percent with no copay. Primary Care covers office visits, urgent care, telemedicine, and chronic care management — the bread and butter of any health plan. Specialist Care includes cardiology, dermatology, orthopedics, neurology, and endocrinology — these usually require referrals and have higher copays. Emergency Services covers emergency room visits, ambulance transport, and trauma care — with copays waived if admitted. Hospital and Inpatient includes overnight stays, ICU care, inpatient surgery, and newborn care — the highest-cost category. Outpatient and Surgical covers same-day surgery, diagnostic imaging, lab tests, and therapy sessions. Mental Health includes therapy, psychiatric care, and substance abuse treatment. Maternity covers prenatal visits, delivery, and postnatal care. Rehabilitation includes physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy. And Diagnostics covers advanced imaging like MRI and CT scans, genetic testing, and specialized lab work. In the Coverage Mapper, each encounter type connects to a benefit definition that specifies copays, coinsurance, prior authorization, and visit limits — creating a complete coverage matrix for the product.
