In this deep-dive, we walk through the PCG4 configuration wizard step by step. Step one is Insurer Details — this is where you enter the insurance company name, registration number, regulatory body, and contact information. Every configuration is tied to exactly one insurer. Step two is Product Details — here you define the product name, product code, effective dates, renewal terms, and product type such as individual, group, or family. Step three is Create Plans — this is where you build plan tiers. Typical configurations have four tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum, each with different coverage levels and pricing bands. Step four is Base Configuration — you set global rules that apply across all plans: annual limits, lifetime maximums, deductibles, out-of-pocket caps, and coinsurance percentages. Step five is Encounters — this is where you select which of the forty-four encounter types the product covers, organized across ten clinical categories from preventive care to diagnostics and lab. Step six is Benefits Setup — for each selected encounter, you define copayment amounts, coinsurance rates, visit limits, prior authorization requirements, and waiting periods. Step seven is Plan-Specific Overrides — any base rule can be overridden per plan. For example, Gold might have zero copay for preventive care while Bronze has a twenty-five dollar copay. Step eight is Review and Publish — the system validates all rules, checks for conflicts, and shows a complete summary before submitting for approval through the Maker-Checker-Publisher workflow.
